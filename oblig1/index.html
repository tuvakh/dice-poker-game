Spanish Dice Poker

A strategic two-player dice game built with Web Components

How to Play

The Game

Spanish Dice Poker is a two-player dice game where each player rolls five "poker dice" (marked with A, K, Q, J, 8, 7) to form the best poker hand. Players take turns rolling up to three times, holding any dice between rolls to achieve their best hand. The player with the highest-ranked hand wins the round. The first player to win the required number of rounds wins the match.

Hand Rankings (High to Low)

  1. Repóker (Five of a Kind) — All five dice show the same face
  2. Póker (Four of a Kind) — Four dice match; one different
  3. Full (Full House) — Three of one face + two of another
  4. Escalera (Straight) — All five dice different: 7-8-J-Q-K or 8-J-Q-K-A
  5. Trío (Three of a Kind) — Three dice match; two different
  6. Doble Pareja (Two Pairs) — Two pairs + one different die
  7. Pareja (One Pair) — One pair + three different dice
  8. Carta Alta (High Card) — All different faces, no combinations

Game Rules

  • Face Order: A > K > Q > J > 8 > 7 (Ace is highest)
  • Rolling: Each player gets one automatic roll, then may re-roll up to 2 more times
  • Holding: After each roll, hold any dice you want to keep for the next roll
  • Match Length: Best-of-3, 5, or 7 (first to 2, 3, or 4 wins)
  • Straights: Optional rule (can be disabled). Only two sequences allowed: 7-8-J-Q-K or 8-J-Q-K-A

Getting Started

  1. Set player names and match length (best-of-3, 5, or 7)
  2. Player 1 starts the round and rolls their dice
  3. Choose which dice to hold and re-roll the rest (up to 2 more rolls)
  4. Player 2 takes their turn with the same flow
  5. The board compares both hands and announces the round winner
  6. Click "Start Next Round" to continue or see match results when someone wins
